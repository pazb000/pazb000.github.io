Seasons are a result of Earth's axial tilt of about 23.5 degrees and its elliptical orbit around the Sun. This tilt causes different parts of the Earth to receive varying amounts of sunlight at different times of the year, leading to the changing seasons. Solstices and equinoxes mark key points in this annual cycle, with the tilt of the Earth's axis determining the angle and intensity of sunlight, influencing the onset of spring,summer, fall and winter.

4 seasons are inevitable yet unpredictable!
